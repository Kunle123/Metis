# Metis art-direction browser validation

## Brief
- The main brief artifact now reads as the dominant plane: the document body is visibly brighter and more continuous than the right-hand status rail.
- The active navigation state is stronger and easier to spot in the sidebar.
- Readiness colors interrupt clearly against the dark background, especially the blue updated state and the amber open-gap state.
- The support rail is darker and recedes appropriately, though the right column still feels slightly dense because several stacked status blocks carry similar weight.

## Compare
- The compare screen now has clearer hierarchy: the central change ledger dominates and the circulation panel recedes as a darker secondary rail.
- The prior/current posture split reads more clearly, with the current posture panel carrying a stronger highlighted state without becoming flashy.
- Updated and validation states are easier to distinguish on dark surfaces than before.
- Remaining issue: the top disposition summary in the right rail is improved but still somewhat text-dense at first glance.

## Gaps
- The Gaps screen now reads as a true primary ledger, with open items carrying enough amber emphasis to stand apart from resolved items.
- The darker right rail stays secondary and no longer competes with the clarification rows.
- The question card inside each gap is clearer and easier to scan than before.
- Remaining issue: the ledger rows are still text-heavy, so very long gap prompts may need future condensation or collapsible handling.

## Export
- The package-selection column is clearly dominant, and the selected package highlight is calm but distinct.
- The preview rail now feels appropriately secondary because the surrounding support surface is darker than the central package pane.
- Readiness colors remain legible in the package list and the circulation checks.
- Remaining issue: the preview card itself is intentionally bright, so on first glance it can briefly compete with the selected package row before the eye settles on the main left column.

## Setup
- The intake form now clearly dominates the page, with the form fields reading as the brightest working surface.
- The draft-posture card recedes more effectively than before, which helps the page feel less split between equal columns.
- The active navigation state is strong enough to guide the eye without breaking the board-safe tone.
- Remaining issue: the support checklist block inside the main pane is still slightly heavy and could be reduced further if the form needs even more emphasis.

## Sources
- The source ledger now has stronger row hierarchy and better contrast against the surrounding page frame.
- The section-posture rail is clearly darker and secondary, which makes the evidence list feel like the main task area.
- The needs-validation and source-conflict states are now easier to distinguish at a glance on the dark background.
- Remaining issue: the top summary strip is improved, but the tier badges still carry similar visual weight and could be normalized later if even calmer scanning is needed.
